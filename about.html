Our History

In the mid-19th century, Turin, Italy, was a place of great social change. It was here that Saint John Bosco, a humble priest, saw the struggle of young workers and street children.

He didn't just offer them food; he offered them a future. By establishing the Salesian Society, he created a network of schools and homes that transformed thousands of lives.
